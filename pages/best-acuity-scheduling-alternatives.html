Why Businesses Search for an Acuity Scheduling Alternative

Acuity Scheduling is a capable scheduling tool, but it is not the right fit for every business. Here are the most common reasons businesses look for an alternative:

  • No free plan. Plans start at $16/month billed annually, with only a 7-day free trial (as of March 2026). Businesses that want to test a platform without a deadline hit a wall immediately.
  • Limited payment gateways. Acuity supports Stripe, Square, and PayPal (as of March 2026). If you operate in Southeast Asia, the Middle East, or anywhere that relies on regional processors like Mollie, Adyen, or Alipay, those three gateways may not serve your clients well.
  • Expensive HIPAA compliance. Healthcare providers who need a signed BAA must subscribe to the Premium plan at $39/month billed annually (as of March 2026). That is a significant cost just to meet a baseline compliance requirement.
  • Key features gated to higher plans. SMS reminders, memberships, packages, and gift certificates require the Standard plan ($27/month annual) or above (as of March 2026).
  • Squarespace ecosystem lock-in. If you use WordPress, Wix, a custom site, or no website at all, some of Acuity's tightest integrations do not apply to you.

These are structural limitations that affect specific types of businesses. The alternatives below address one or more of these gaps.

Each platform has genuine strengths for different use cases. We cover features, pricing, and the specific gaps each one fills.

1

SimplyBook.me

Best for: Service businesses that need flexible payments, multi-channel booking, and affordable compliance.

2

Calendly

Best for: Teams that need simple meeting scheduling with calendar-first workflows.

Calendly is the most widely known scheduling tool, primarily designed for meeting coordination rather than service-business appointment booking. It excels at one-on-one meeting scheduling, round-robin assignments, and team availability management (as of March 2026).

Key strengths:

  • Free plan available (1 event type, basic integrations)
  • Strong calendar integrations with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud
  • Team scheduling features including collective and round-robin booking
  • Routing forms to qualify and direct leads before booking
  • Integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and other CRMs

Limitations:

Calendly is built around calendar scheduling for meetings, not full service-business booking. It does not offer built-in payment processing on the free plan, has no POS system, no loyalty program, and no multi-channel consumer-facing booking page (as of March 2026). If you are a salon, clinic, or fitness studio that needs to accept payments at the time of booking and manage walk-ins, Calendly may feel limited.

Pricing: Free plan available. Professional plan at $10/seat/month billed annually (as of March 2026). Note the per-seat pricing model.

3

Setmore

Best for: Small businesses and solo providers who want a generous free plan with basic payment support.

Setmore offers one of the most feature-complete free plans in the scheduling space, supporting up to 4 users and 200 appointments per month on the Free tier (as of March 2026).

Key strengths:

  • Free plan supports up to 4 staff logins and 200 bookings/month
  • Square and Stripe payment integration on the free plan
  • Social media booking links for Instagram and Facebook
  • Video meeting integration with Teleport (Setmore's built-in video tool)
  • Simple, clean interface suited to small teams

Limitations:

Setmore's free plan caps at 200 appointments/month, and paid plans support only Stripe and Square for payments (as of March 2026). There is no HIPAA compliance option listed. Businesses that need regional payment gateways, advanced marketing tools, or healthcare compliance will likely outgrow Setmore quickly.

Pricing: Free plan available. Pro plan at $5/user/month billed annually. Team plan at $5/user/month for 2+ users (as of March 2026).

4

Square Appointments

Best for: Businesses already in the Square ecosystem that want scheduling tied to their POS.

Square Appointments is part of the broader Square platform and combines appointment scheduling with Square's POS and payment processing. If you already use Square for payments, the scheduling integration is natural and well-executed (as of March 2026).

Key strengths:

  • Free plan for individual users (1 location, unlimited bookings)
  • Deeply integrated with Square POS, invoicing, and payment processing
  • Built-in contract sending and intake forms
  • No-show protection with card-on-file and cancellation fees
  • Instagram and Google booking integrations

Limitations:

Square Appointments processes payments exclusively through Square (as of March 2026). If you need Stripe, PayPal, Mollie, or any other gateway, Square Appointments is not an option. The free plan is limited to a single user at one location. Multi-staff plans start at $29/month per location. There is no HIPAA compliance listed.

Pricing: Free for individuals. Plus plan at $29/month/location, Premium at $69/month/location (as of March 2026).

5

Cal.com

Best for: Developer-friendly teams and businesses that want open-source scheduling.

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling platform that provides full control over the codebase for self-hosted deployments. It is the strongest option for technical teams that want to customize their scheduling infrastructure at the code level (as of March 2026).

Key strengths:

  • Open-source (self-hostable) with the full codebase on GitHub
  • Free plan available for individuals on the hosted version
  • Developer-friendly with a strong API and webhook support
  • Round-robin, collective scheduling, and routing forms
  • Integrations with Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, and Stripe

Limitations:

Self-hosting requires DevOps resources to maintain. The hosted free plan is limited to 1 event type (as of March 2026). There is no built-in POS, loyalty program, or multi-channel booking page for consumer-facing businesses. Payment integrations are limited to Stripe on most plans. HIPAA compliance is not listed.

Pricing: Free hosted plan available. Team plan at $12/user/month. Enterprise pricing is custom (as of March 2026).

Frequently Asked Questions About Acuity Scheduling Alternatives

What are the best Acuity Scheduling alternatives in 2026?

The top five Acuity Scheduling alternatives are SimplyBook.me, Calendly, Setmore, Square Appointments, and Cal.com. Each serves a different type of business. SimplyBook.me is the strongest option for service businesses that need flexible payments and affordable compliance. Calendly suits meeting-focused teams. Setmore offers a generous free plan for small businesses. Square Appointments works well inside the Square ecosystem. Cal.com appeals to developer-friendly teams that want open-source control.

Is SimplyBook.me better than Acuity Scheduling for small businesses?

SimplyBook.me offers a free plan with no time limit, which Acuity does not (as of March 2026). For small businesses that want to start without a subscription, that is a significant difference. SimplyBook.me also includes loyalty programs, gift cards, and memberships on all paid plans, features that require Acuity's Standard plan ($27/month) or above. However, Acuity offers unlimited bookings on all plans, which may matter more if your volume is high relative to your budget.

Does SimplyBook.me support HIPAA compliance?

Yes. SimplyBook.me offers HIPAA compliance with BAA signing on the Standard plan at €24.90/month billed annually (as of March 2026). This includes encrypted data handling, access controls, and audit logging required for protected health information. Acuity requires the Premium plan ($39/month annual) for the same compliance level.

Can I migrate my client data from Acuity Scheduling to SimplyBook.me?

Yes. Export your client list and appointment history from Acuity as CSV files, then import them into SimplyBook.me through the admin dashboard. The import process handles client names, contact details, and appointment records. Most businesses complete the full migration in a few hours.

Which Acuity alternative has the most payment gateways?

SimplyBook.me supports 40+ payment gateways, more than any other platform on this list (as of March 2026). This includes Stripe, PayPal, Square, Mollie, Adyen, Alipay, Worldpay, and dozens of regional processors. Calendly supports Stripe and PayPal. Setmore supports Stripe and Square. Square Appointments supports only Square. Cal.com supports Stripe.

Is there a free alternative to Acuity Scheduling?

Yes. SimplyBook.me, Calendly, Setmore, Square Appointments, and Cal.com all offer free plans. The most generous free plan for team use is Setmore (4 users, 200 bookings/month). The most feature-rich free plan for solo providers is Square Appointments (unlimited bookings, 1 user). SimplyBook.me's free plan (1 provider, 50 bookings/month) includes the core booking system with no time limit.

How does Calendly compare to Acuity Scheduling?

Calendly and Acuity serve different use cases. Calendly is optimized for meeting scheduling (sales calls, interviews, team coordination), while Acuity is built for service-business appointments (salons, clinics, fitness studios). Calendly's free plan offers 1 event type with unlimited bookings. Its per-seat pricing ($10/seat/month Professional) scales differently than Acuity's per-business model. Calendly has stronger CRM integrations (Salesforce, HubSpot), but weaker payment and service-business features compared to both Acuity and SimplyBook.me (as of March 2026).

Is Cal.com a good Acuity Scheduling alternative?

Cal.com is a strong alternative if you have developer resources and want full control over your scheduling infrastructure. Its open-source model lets you self-host and customize the platform at the code level. For non-technical teams, the hosted version works similarly to other scheduling tools but with fewer built-in features for service businesses (no POS, loyalty program, or multi-channel booking page). Cal.com is best suited for tech companies, developer teams, and businesses with specific customization requirements (as of March 2026).
