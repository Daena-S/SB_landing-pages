## Why Businesses Search for an Acuity Scheduling Alternative

Acuity Scheduling is a capable scheduling tool, but it is not the right fit for every business. Here are the most common reasons businesses look for an alternative:

- **No free plan.** Acuity Scheduling does not offer a free tier. Plans start at $16/month billed annually ($20/month billed monthly), with only a 7-day free trial (as of March 2026, source: acuityscheduling.com/signup.php). Businesses that want to test a platform without a deadline, or solo providers who need basic scheduling at zero cost, hit a wall immediately.

- **Limited payment gateways.** Acuity supports Stripe, Square, and PayPal (as of March 2026, source: acuityscheduling.com/integrations). If you operate in Southeast Asia, the Middle East, parts of Africa, or anywhere that relies on regional processors like Mollie, Adyen, Alipay, or Worldpay, those three gateways may not serve your clients well.

- **Expensive HIPAA compliance.** Healthcare providers who need a signed Business Associate Agreement (BAA) must subscribe to the Premium plan at $39/month billed annually or $49/month billed monthly (as of March 2026, source: acuityscheduling.com/signup.php). That is a significant cost just to meet a baseline compliance requirement.

- **Key features gated to higher plans.** SMS reminders, memberships, packages, and gift certificates require the Standard plan ($27/month annual) or above (as of March 2026, source: acuityscheduling.com/signup.php). On the Starter plan, you are limited to email reminders and basic scheduling.

- **Squarespace ecosystem lock-in.** Acuity integrates deeply with Squarespace, which is a benefit if you already use Squarespace for your website. But if you use WordPress, Wix, a custom site, or no website at all, some of Acuity's tightest integrations do not apply to you.

These are not subjective complaints. They are structural limitations that affect specific types of businesses. The alternatives below address one or more of these gaps.

### 2. Calendly

**Best for:** Teams that need simple meeting scheduling with calendar-first workflows.

Calendly is the most widely known scheduling tool, primarily designed for meeting coordination rather than service-business appointment booking. It excels at one-on-one meeting scheduling, round-robin assignments, and team availability management (as of March 2026, source: calendly.com/features).

**Key strengths:**

- Free plan available (1 event type, basic integrations)
- Strong calendar integrations with Google Calendar, Outlook, and iCloud
- Team scheduling features including collective and round-robin booking
- Routing forms to qualify and direct leads before booking
- Integrations with Salesforce, HubSpot, and other CRMs

**Limitations:** Calendly is built around calendar scheduling for meetings, not full service-business booking. It does not offer built-in payment processing on the free plan, has no POS system, no loyalty program, and no multi-channel consumer-facing booking page (as of March 2026, source: calendly.com/pricing). If you are a salon, clinic, or fitness studio that needs to accept payments at the time of booking and manage walk-ins, Calendly may feel limited.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Professional plan at $10/seat/month billed annually (as of March 2026, source: calendly.com/pricing). Note the per-seat pricing model, which scales differently than per-business pricing.

### 3. Setmore

**Best for:** Small businesses and solo providers who want a generous free plan with basic payment support.

Setmore offers one of the most feature-complete free plans in the scheduling space, supporting up to 4 users and 200 appointments per month on the Free tier (as of March 2026, source: setmore.com/pricing). It covers online booking pages, email reminders, and Stripe/Square payments.

**Key strengths:**

- Free plan supports up to 4 staff logins and 200 bookings/month
- Square and Stripe payment integration on the free plan
- Social media booking links for Instagram and Facebook
- Video meeting integration with Teleport (Setmore's built-in video tool)
- Simple, clean interface suited to small teams

**Limitations:** Setmore's free plan caps at 200 appointments/month, and paid plans support only Stripe and Square for payments (as of March 2026, source: setmore.com/pricing). There is no HIPAA compliance option listed on the pricing or features pages (as of March 2026, source: setmore.com). Businesses that need regional payment gateways, advanced marketing tools, or healthcare compliance will likely outgrow Setmore quickly.

**Pricing:** Free plan available. Pro plan at $5/user/month billed annually. Team plan at $5/user/month for 2+ users (as of March 2026, source: setmore.com/pricing).

If you need Stripe, PayPal, Mollie, or any other gateway, Square Appointments is not an option. The free plan is limited to a single user at one location. Multi-staff plans start at $29/month per location (as of March 2026, source: squareup.com/us/en/appointments/pricing). There is no HIPAA compliance listed on the features page.

**Pricing:** Free for individuals. Plus plan at $29/month/location, Premium at $69/month/location (as of March 2026, source: squareup.com/us/en/appointments/pricing).

Cal.com is an open-source scheduling platform that provides full control over the codebase for self-hosted deployments. It is the strongest option for technical teams that want to customize their scheduling infrastructure at the code level (as of March 2026, source: cal.com).

**Key strengths:**

- Open-source (self-hostable) with the full codebase on GitHub
- Free plan available for individuals on the hosted version
- Developer-friendly with a strong API and webhook support
- Round-robin, collective scheduling, and routing forms
- Integrations with Google Calendar, Outlook, Zoom, and Stripe

**Limitations:** Cal.com's strength is also its limitation for non-technical users. Self-hosting requires DevOps resources to maintain. Payment integrations are limited to Stripe on most plans (as of March 2026, source: cal.com/pricing). HIPAA compliance is not listed on the pricing or features pages.

**Pricing:** Free hosted plan available. Team plan at $12/user/month. Enterprise pricing is custom (as of March 2026, source: cal.com/pricing).

## How to Switch From Acuity Scheduling to SimplyBook.me

Switching scheduling platforms does not require downtime or lost data. Here is the process:

1. **Export your data from Acuity.** Download your client list, appointment history, and any package/membership records from Acuity's export tools. Acuity supports CSV export for client data and appointments.

2. **Set up your SimplyBook.me account.** Start with a free plan or a 14-day trial of any paid plan. Configure your services, providers, working hours, and payment gateway. Import your client list via CSV upload.

3. **Redirect your booking links.** Update your website, social media profiles, and email signatures to point to your new SimplyBook.me booking page. If you used Acuity's scheduling link, set up a redirect so existing links still work for returning clients.

The entire process takes most businesses less than an afternoon. No developer needed.
